# Clarify: CBT 7-Column Thought Record Journal

## Project Overview

**Clarify** is a private, modern, offline-first Progressive Web Application (PWA) designed to facilitate the classic 7-column thought record exercise—a cornerstone cognitive restructuring technique in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). 

By guiding users through a structured, step-by-step wizard (Situation, Emotions, Automatic Thoughts, Evidence For, Evidence Against, Alternative Thoughts, and Re-rating), the application helps individuals systematically identify, challenge, and reframe dysfunctional or distorted thinking patterns. 

Built using a mobile-first philosophy with pure HTML, Vanilla CSS, and native JavaScript, the app operates entirely on the user's device. All thought records, emotional ratings, and analytics are persisted securely inside local device storage (`localStorage`), ensuring complete user privacy without external server dependencies.

## Scientific Validation

Cognitive restructuring via the 7-column thought record is one of the most widely researched and clinically validated interventions in contemporary clinical psychology. The technique is rooted in Aaron T. Beck’s cognitive model, which posits that psychological distress is maintained by cognitive distortions—biased, automatic ways of processing information. 

### Key Clinical Findings & Mechanisms

1. **Efficacy in Symptom Reduction**: Large-scale meta-analyses consistently demonstrate that CBT and its cognitive restructuring components produce significant, long-lasting reductions in symptoms of generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), major depressive disorder (MDD), social anxiety, and stress (Hofmann et al., 2012).
2. **Cognitive Restructuring vs. Active Controls**: Component analyses show that systematically writing down automatic thoughts and evaluating them against objective, factual evidence (the core of the 7-column thought record) significantly outperforms non-structured journaling or passive reflection in restructuring core beliefs and lowering emotional distress (Dobson & Dozois, 2019).
3. **Efficacy of Digital Self-Directed Interventions**: Research into digitized CBT tools demonstrates that self-directed web and mobile applications utilizing structured cognitive restructuring exercises are highly effective in reducing subclinical anxiety and depression, serving as powerful standalone tools or therapeutic adjuncts (Lattie et al., 2019).
